Education

My undergraduate degree is very diverse and sparks the question of "What are you going to do with that?"

Below I list the benefits of a dual degree in Economics and Spanish with a minor in Business Administration:

Economics and Business Administration

Through my Economics and Business Studies, I have learned several hard and soft skills that I feel have prepared me for future success.

My four years thus far have provided me with a diverse skill set that has shaped me into a confident and impactful member of our campus community and the communities I have entered since being at Washington & Jefferson College.

Technical Skills

  • Quantitative and Qualitative Research Collection
  • Statistical Analysis using Ordinary Least Squares Methods
  • Data management and maintenance using Microsoft Excel

Soft Skills:

  • Communicate with real businesses in the Washington Community about their business structure and operations
  • Lead class discussions on historical Economic theory
  • Present high-level research, literature review, and data and methodology techniques to peers and professors

How have Economics and Business programs complemented each other for me?

I have found value in the mixture of both the back-end and front-end of businesses and the economy. In my economics courses, I have developed a strong analytical and mathematical-minded skill set for problem-resolution scenarios. In my business courses, I have learned intuitive thinking that has made me more creative, innovative, and versatile when I am confronted with new challenges.

Purpose/Goals:

For a long time, I was also asking myself the question: "What am I going to do with this degree?" However, I trusted myself, my professors, and my mentors along the way as I developed my vision for my future.

My goal is to pursue higher education and complete a Master's program in Economic Development at the University of Carlos III in Madrid, Spain. My studies and real-world experiencs showed me that I want to immerse myself cultrually into a business environment where I am also given the opportunity to be an impactful community member.

The completion of this program will open up the following opportunities for me:

  • Connections with Global Non-Profit and NGO organizations
  • Opportunity to extend my studies at the University of Lunde in Sweden
  • Contribute to economic development initiatives such as public transportation and education projects
